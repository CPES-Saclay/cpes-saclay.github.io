  <Conference
    titre="When machines think and decide - free will and responsibility in the age of AI"
    intervenant="Julien Gobin"
    partenaire="HEC Paris"
    date="December 2025"
    description="AI revives, in a very concrete way, age-old questions. Drawing on the metaphor of the Tower of Babel, the speaker examines the promise of a single language, efficiency, prediction, optimisation, mathematisation, grounded in information: does it serve to produce more order, or to seek truth? He then situates this technology within liberal civilisation, built upon free will, autonomy and responsibility (a destiny shaped by free and informed consent). With AI, judgement and decision-making are externalised: a new vision of the human being emerges, in which the exercise of our responsibility slips away at the risk of erasing the very pillars of our civilisation."

  <Conference
    titre="Digital health: contributions and perspectives"
    intervenant="Clémence Marty-Chastan"
    date="September 2024 and December 2025"
    partenaire="HEC Paris"
    description="An overview of e-health (teleconsultation, remote monitoring, tele-expertise, tele-care, robotics): a tool to complement rather than replace care, to address medical deserts and reduce hospitals' carbon footprint. Yet it relies on a fragile relationship of trust, sensitive and scattered data (SNDS, unified patient record), a demanding regulatory framework (GDPR, HDS, CNIL), and persistent obstacles: interoperability, AI bias, the digital divide and the slow pace of integration in the sector."

    intervenant="Bénédicte Faivre-Tavignot"
    partenaire="HEC Paris"
    date="September 2024 and December 2025"
    description="Inclusive economics as a response to social fragmentation, a major risk alongside climate and geopolitics.   <Conference
    titre="Democracies in crisis and populism"
    intervenant="Yann Algan"
    partenaire="HEC Paris"
    date="2023 - 2024"
    description="An analysis of the crisis of liberal-representative democracies through the lens of populism. The symptoms are multiple: rising abstention, the erosion of governing parties, declining unionisation. The satisfaction curve flattens among younger generations. Populism, hard to define, refers in its pejorative sense to a corrupt elite facing a pure people. Resentment is framed as 'the poison of democracy'."
  />
  <Conference
    titre="Data journalism, fact-checking and data integration"
    intervenant="Nelly Barret"
    partenaire="CEDAR, Inria"
    date="2023 - 2024"
    description="Presentation of StatCheck, an automated fact-checking tool: a warehouse of statistical data coupled with a search engine and textual analysis to detect persuasive techniques in the media (binary classification or multi-class across 13 techniques). Broader view on a system for integrating heterogeneous data with unified access through a mediator, a warehouse or a data lake (graph representation, compact summaries, SQL queries). Data journalism is defined as the intersection of facts, raw data and computing skills."

    partenaire="GHNE"
    date="2023 - 2024"
    description="The hospital sector accounts for 11.5% of GDP, including 5% for public hospitals, but suffers from a structural shortage of staff. The conference posits ontological trust as the sine qua non of any digital transformation: without it, action becomes violent or anarchic. Illustrated by hospitals' refusal to use GDP as a steering indicator, and by the inability to treat 40% of patients due to a lack of doctors."
